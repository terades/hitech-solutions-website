# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Production managers, plant managers, and operations leads at German mid-sized manufacturing companies (Mittelstand, roughly 50–500 employees). They work daily with fragmented toolchains — ERP for order data, Excel for planning, disconnected shop floor systems — and make production decisions based on assumptions rather than live data. They are pragmatic and skeptical: they've seen expensive digitalization projects fail to survive contact with the real factory floor. A second audience is IT/OT decision-makers at the same companies who greenlight projects and care about integration capability (ERP, OPC UA, Azure) and total cost of commitment.

## Product Purpose

HI-Tech Solutions builds pragmatic digitalization solutions for mid-size production companies — connecting ERP systems, shop floor machines, and production planning into one coherent picture. The differentiator is the combination of real production knowledge and clean software engineering: no generic MES rollout, no consulting bloat, no 18-month projects. The promise is a working MVP in weeks, starting from the actual bottleneck. Success looks like a production manager who can finally see what's happening on Linie 03 in real time, and act on it.

## Brand Personality

Innovativ · Verlässlich · Direkt

Voice is peer-to-peer, not vendor-to-customer. Copy reads the way a senior engineer would explain the problem to a plant manager over coffee — concrete, confident, no buzzwords. "Die im Werk wirklich funktioniert" is the guiding phrase: it tells the reader we know what it's like when things don't work.

## Anti-references

- **SAP / Siemens / Bosch** — corporate polish, distant enterprise scale, impersonal. Avoid anything that feels like a Fortune 500 vendor deck.
- **Generic SaaS cream** — warm tinted near-white backgrounds, rounded cards with icon + heading + text repeated in a grid, tracked eyebrow labels above every section. The saturated AI landing-page aesthetic reads as template, not expertise.
- **Consulting-speak websites** — abstract diagrams, stock photos of people shaking hands, words like "transformation", "synergy", "end-to-end digital journey."
- **Numbered section eyebrows (01 / 02 / 03)** used as structural default — already partially present in the friction-flow diagram where it's earned; not acceptable as a site-wide scaffold.

## Design Principles

1. **Fertigung zuerst** — every section earns its place by reflecting something a production environment actually deals with. If a section could appear on a generic B2B software site, rework it until it couldn't.
2. **Zeigen, nicht behaupten** — real dashboards, actual OEE numbers, concrete timelines. Credibility comes from specifics, not adjectives.
3. **Mittelstandsgleichhöhe** — speak at the same level as the customer, not above them. Technical confidence without corporate distance; approachable without being casual.
4. **Pragmatisch bis ins Detail** — no decoration for its own sake. Every visual element either communicates or guides. Whitespace is deliberate, not filler.
5. **Vertrauen vor Impression** — concrete case signals, real founder faces, honest scoping. Trust is harder to earn with animation than with specifics.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA for all text and interactive elements. Reduced-motion alternatives for all animations (already partially in place via `prefers-reduced-motion`). No color-only information encoding — all status indicators use icon or label alongside color.
